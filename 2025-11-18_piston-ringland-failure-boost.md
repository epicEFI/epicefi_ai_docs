# Piston Ringland Failure from Excessive Boost

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2025-11-18 | Channel: 1411925674498719775*
*Contributors: @CarolinaCumberland, @ToyotaTerrorist*

## Summary

@CarolinaCumberland reported cracking ring lands on 3 out of 6 pistons due to running excessive boost pressure with worn stock rings on pistons that have a known safe boost limit. @ToyotaTerrorist offered guidance on ring gap and detonation as root causes.

## Details

### Failure Description

- **Pistons affected:** 3 of 6 ring lands cracked
- **Boost pressure at time of failure:** 18 psi
- **Known safe range for these pistons:** 10–14 psi
- **Contributing factor:** Old, worn-out stock rings with loose gaps

The combination of running well above the piston's rated pressure threshold and already-worn ring gaps led to mechanical failure.

### Root Cause Analysis

**Loose ring gaps** are a known failure mode for ring lands. Worn rings that no longer seat properly allow blow-by gases to pressurize the land area, which can cause cracking even at moderate boost levels.

@ToyotaTerrorist noted:
> "Gap them properly and you'll never break ring lands unless you get into detonation."

This highlights two primary causes of ring land failure:
1. **Improper ring gap** (too loose allows pressure build-up; too tight causes thermal binding)
2. **Detonation** — abnormal combustion events produce sharp pressure spikes that can crack ring lands regardless of proper gap settings

### Key Takeaways

- Running stock pistons above their rated boost pressure (10–14 psi for these particular pistons) significantly increases failure risk.
- Worn or improperly gapped rings dramatically reduce the safe boost threshold.
- Proper ring gap sizing is a prerequisite for any boosted application.
- Detonation protection (correct ignition timing, adequate fuel enrichment, knock detection) is essential to prevent ring land damage even with properly prepared pistons.
