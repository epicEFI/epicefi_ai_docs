# Wiring Harness Manufacturing: Crimping, Outsourcing, and Materials

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2025-11-18 | Channel: 1356732771325968630*
*Contributors: @Joesphan, @BroKingscooters, @Lysdex*

## Summary

Discussion of practical approaches to producing wiring harnesses for rusEFI/epicEFI installations, covering outsourced crimping services, quality control considerations when sourcing from overseas manufacturers, in-house custom bundles, and the preferred wire materials (Tefzel and Raychem) used in professional automotive wiring harnesses.

## Details

### Outsourced Crimping vs. In-House Production

Custom wiring harnesses built in-house allow full control over wire gauge, bundle routing, and connector selection, but labor cost is high. @Joesphan proposed finding an outside shop or operator to handle the cut-strip-crimp steps:

> "I bet I can find someone to run a crimping machine to just do cut, strip, and crimp and the install experience will be miles ahead of bare wire."

@BroKingscooters agreed the approach could reduce cost, but raised quality control (QC) as the key concern when using a third-party or overseas supplier:

> "I am sure with a bit of sourcing we could find a China company to do them at a reasonable price per part crimp, but then there is the QC of it. Keeping it in house allows you to do custom wire bundles at absurd upcharge."

### Trade-offs Summary

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Outsourced crimping (domestic) | Lower labor cost than full in-house; consistent crimp quality | Less flexibility for custom bundles |
| Outsourced crimping (overseas/China) | Lowest per-crimp cost | QC risk; difficult to verify workmanship |
| In-house production | Full custom bundles; known QC | Highest labor cost |

### Preferred Wire Materials

For high-quality automotive wiring harnesses, @Lysdex noted the target materials are:

- **Tefzel (ETFE):** A fluoropolymer-insulated wire commonly used in motorsport and aerospace wiring harnesses. Very thin insulation, high temperature rating, and excellent resistance to fuel, oil, and solvents.
- **Raychem:** A brand name widely used to refer to Raychem DR-25 heat-shrink tubing and Spec 55 wire, which are the aerospace/motorsport standard for strain relief and wire bundling.

These materials are more expensive than standard automotive wire and heat shrink but provide significantly better durability, especially in engine bay environments with high heat, vibration, and fluid exposure.
