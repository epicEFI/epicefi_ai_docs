# Hydraulic Clutch Bleeding: One-Way Valve and 02M Slave Cylinder

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2025-11-18 | Channel: 1411925674498719775*
*Contributors: @Joesphan, @Lysdex, @Psychedelia*

## Summary

Discussion on hydraulic clutch bleeding techniques, specifically around a one-way clutch system where the master cylinder does not displace enough fluid, and a question about bleeding the VW/Audi 02M gearbox slave cylinder.

## Details

### Master Cylinder Displacement Issue

@Joesphan noted that the clutch master cylinder in their setup does not displace sufficient fluid volume to properly operate a one-way clutch system. This is a known limitation when adapting certain master cylinders to clutch setups they were not originally designed for.

### Valve Technique for Building Pressure

@Lysdex suggested a technique to improve pumping effectiveness when bleeding or priming:
> "It's better to have the valve closed when you pump. Helps build pressure before you open."

This approach — closing the outlet valve during the pump stroke, then opening it — allows pressure to build before fluid is pushed through the line, which can help move air bubbles more effectively.

### Vacuum Bleeding Procedure (Tee Method)

@Joesphan described a vacuum bleeding approach using a tee fitting at the master cylinder:

1. Tee off at the master cylinder
2. Pump/bleed the master until fluid (not air) exits the tee port
3. Attach a hose to the tee
4. Open the ball valve on the hose
5. Proceed with vacuum bleeding from there

This method ensures the master cylinder itself is free of air before the vacuum bleeder is connected downstream.

### 02M Slave Cylinder (VW/Audi)

@Joesphan asked about the correct procedure for bleeding a **02M** (VW/Audi 5/6-speed manual) slave cylinder, specifically requesting input from someone with Audi experience. @Psychedelia asked for clarification on whether the slave cylinder in question is an **internal** or **external** type, as the bleeding procedure differs:

- **External slave cylinder** (CSC mounted outside the bellhousing): Accessible bleeder nipple, standard bleed procedure applies.
- **Internal slave cylinder** (concentric slave cylinder inside the bellhousing): Typically requires removal or a specific bleed sequence due to its location.

The specific 02M variant and its cylinder type were not confirmed in this thread excerpt.
