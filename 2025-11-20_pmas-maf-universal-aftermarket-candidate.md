# PMAS MAF as Universal Aftermarket Mass Airflow Sensor Candidate

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2025-11-20 | Channel: 1411925674498719775*
*Contributors: @Joshan Lu (joshseek_30236_07984), @SanGawku (sangawku)*

## Summary

The community lacks a single go-to universal aftermarket MAF sensor comparable to the GM 3-bar MAP (which is widely adopted for its cost, accuracy, and integrated IAT). PMAS (Performance Mass Air Systems) was recommended as a strong candidate for custom MAF installations, with confirmed use on high-power builds.

## Details

### Background

Joshan Lu raised the common problem of selecting an aftermarket MAF sensor for custom or swap applications: the GM 3-bar MAP sensor has an unofficial "universal default" status due to its cost, accuracy, speed, and integrated intake air temperature (IAT) sensor — no similar universal standard exists for MAF sensors.

Desired attributes for a universal MAF candidate:
- Low cost
- Fast response
- High accuracy
- Integrated IAT (measures intake air temperature in the same housing)

### PMAS Recommendation

SanGawku recommended **PMAS (Performance Mass Air Systems)**: https://pmas-maf.com/

Key points from community experience:
- Used successfully on a **Ford Coyote** build producing **900 whp**
- Available in multiple tube sizes/configurations for different applications
- Pricing noted at approximately **$149**

### Application Context

Joshan Lu's application: a **Toyota 1UZ** (Series 1) running a second-hand MAF that is close to failure. The PMAS sensor was accepted as a viable replacement candidate.

### Comparison to GM 3-bar

Unlike the MAP sensor space, there is no universally adopted aftermarket MAF. PMAS fills part of this gap with:
- Calibrated transfer functions
- Multiple diameter options to fit different intake tubes
- Support for high-power applications (validated to 900 whp on Coyote)
