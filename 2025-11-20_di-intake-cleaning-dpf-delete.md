# Intake Cleaning Method After DI Delete / DPF Removal

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2025-11-20 | Channel: 1356732771325968630*
*Contributors: fast335xi (@fast335xi)*

## Summary

Practical method for cleaning the intake system after a direct injection (DI) delete or diesel particulate filter (DPF) removal, using compressed air and a modified compressor tester hose.

## Details

**Method:**
- With the engine and intake assembly still together (no need to disassemble), locate the intake entry point.
- Drill and tap a port if needed to create an air inlet point ("drill/tap with everything together").
- Modify a **compression tester hose** to fit a standard air compressor fitting.
- Insert the hose and blow compressed air through the intake to clear out carbon deposits, soot, and debris dislodged by the DPF or DI delete.

**Advantages of this approach:**
- Avoids a full intake disassembly, saving labor time.
- The compressed air effectively pushes debris out through the intake path without requiring solvent cleaners.
- The modified compressor tester hose is a low-cost tool made from commonly available parts.

**Context:**
On engines with direct injection, significant carbon buildup occurs on intake valves and ports (since there is no fuel wash). After a DI delete or during DPF removal work, cleaning the intake before reassembly prevents debris from entering the cylinders. This technique is a DIY-friendly alternative to walnut blasting or full intake removal.
