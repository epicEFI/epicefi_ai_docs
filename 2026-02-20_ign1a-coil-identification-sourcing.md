# IGN1A Ignition Coil: Identification, Origin, and Sourcing

*Source: rusEFI Discord, 2026-02-20 | Channel: 1411925674498719775*
*Contributors: @MykkH, @jeybee, @ZeeKay*

## Summary

The IGN1A is a widely used aftermarket ignition coil in the standalone ECU community. It originates from Mercury Marine's Optimax outboard engine line and was never fitted to a standard OEM road vehicle. Modern availability comes almost entirely from Chinese clone manufacturers, and significant price differences exist between branded resellers (e.g., Haltech) and direct-from-manufacturer purchasing. The Yunyi brand is considered the better-quality clone.

## Details

### Origin

- The IGN1A coil was originally developed for **Mercury Marine Optimax** outboard engines.
- It was **not used as OE equipment on any standard road vehicle**, so there is no donor car to pull OE units from.
- The coils now sold under the "IGN1A" name by aftermarket suppliers (Haltech and others) are **Chinese clones** of the original Mercury Marine design.

### Clone Manufacturers

Two main Chinese manufacturers produce IGN1A clones:

- **Yunyi Ignition Coil Co.** — considered the higher-quality option
- **Whenzu Rulan** — an alternative source

Yunyi coils use **Infineon or Fairchild/NXP IGBTs** internally and are rated to survive dwell times of **2.5–3.5 ms without thermal failure**.

### Pricing

- Haltech (branded reseller): approximately **£73 per coil**
- Direct from manufacturer (e.g., Yunyi): approximately **£22 per coil** for the same item

### Related Coil Options

The conversation was part of a broader discussion on coil options for aftermarket builds. Other coils mentioned in context:

- **R35 GTR coils** — noted as a popular choice; VQ-engine coils may be equivalent and easier to source than R35-specific units.
- **Bosch Gen 2 / Gen 3 EV1-connector injectors** — referenced alongside; many 1990s BMWs used Bosch Gen 2 injectors, and 2000s Fords used Bosch Gen 3. Both are considered near-universal for adapting into custom builds.
